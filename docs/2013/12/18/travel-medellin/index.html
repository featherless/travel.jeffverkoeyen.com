Planning Notes: Medellin, Colombia

Friday, December 06, 2013 17:27

http://wikitravel.org/en/Medell%C3%ADn

The City of Eternal Spring and capital of the Antioquia department is famous for having a large textile industry, which produces top quality clothing that is sent all over the world. It’s also the birthplace of master painter Fernando Botero, therefore it houses the great majority of his works.

http://www.quora.com/South-America/What-are-some-cool-places-to-go-in-South-America

The wealthiest and second largest city in Colombia located high in the Andes. One of the most beautiful big cities in South America. Advanced subway system. Spring-like weather year-round. Very friendly inhabitants. Botero square in the downtown area features fascinating sculptures from one of Colombia’s most celebrated artists. Also, fun to be had in the Zona Rosa/Parque Lleras area of El Poblado (the wealthy area of Medellin).

To Do

  • Festival of Lights going on from Dec 7 to the second week of January.
  • Cerro Nutibara for a panoramic view of the city.
  • Paisa Road 30,000 COP Pablo Escobar tour.
  • Zona Rosa is the night life district.
  • Museo Interactivo just west off the Alpujarra station.

Getting In

  • There are shuttles that run from the airport to downtown for 7500 COP and take one hour.
  • Collectivo costa 11,000 COP and takes 45 minutes.

Our exact location is Calle 11a No. 42-05 Medellin

From the ‘El Poblado’ park, you just need to ask for the ‘Pomona’ supermarket which is located at one of the corner of the park and we are located just behind it.

To reach the park, you can either:

  • take the metro and get off at the station ‘El Poblado’. It is a 10 minutes walk between the park and the station or a $2 ride by cab.
  • take a taxi from the airport international ($20)
  • take a taxi or bus from the bus terminal del Sur, just ask if the bus goes to El Poblado.

getting anywhere of interest requires a 15 minute walk to the metro station and then 3 stops/a 15-minute ride to the main metro line of Medellin

Shamrock Bar & Grill

Wednesday, December 18, 2013 20:10

We found this place on AirBnB the night before we left for Medellin. The rooms are built over an Irish Pub that Zach (the host) manages. We’re glad we checked out AirBnB for this place because it’s not listed elsewhere as a hotel/bnb.

We ended up staying here for a few days while we explored Medellin and met some of the other guys staying there more long-term. One owned a giant fluffy himalayan cat named Bear who would come visit our room through the window.

Thaico

Wednesday, December 18, 2013 20:40

This is my tripadvisor review:

This place is hardly worth the time to go to, much less write a review, but I felt it necessary to attempt to further this establishment’s drunken stumble down to tripadvisor’s gutters.

First off, this place doesn’t serve Thai food other than by name. Any of the traditional flavors you’re used to expecting from Thai food are conspicuously missing from every plate. To top it off, the flavors they DO have make you want to vomit during, immediately after, and for the duration of the evening following.

This terrible experience is compounded by the miserable staff. Don’t expect to get any service while you’re here without flagging someone down, and, when you do get it, don’t expect them to listen to any complaints about the quality of their food. Our waitress, upon being informed of the meat tasting like gutter, simply nodded and walked away.

Don’t waste your time or money on this place.
